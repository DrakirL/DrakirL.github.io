**Shields:** Small. <br>
**Focus:** Primal. <br>
**Training:** Choose one from Herbalism, Survival, Folklore, or Nature. <br>

## Starting Equipment
+ A Simple Melee Weapon of your choice.
+ Either a small wooden shield or a sling and a bag containing 20 rocks ("sling bullets").
+ A component pouch containing up to 10 gp worth of materials.
+ A Primal Focus of your choice. May be merged with the weapon or worn.

Your Intelligence becomes your Spellcasting Ability.

### Level 1: Druid Subclass
You choose a Druid Subclass and gain its Level 1 Features.
Your subclass comes with a list of Circle Spells. You learn these Spells at the defined levels, and they count as Circle Spells for you, even if they don't normally. (PHRASING?)

### Level 2: Druidic Secrets
You learn to read the secret signs of the Druids, which are hide simple descriptive messages made by shaping glyphs into cracks in stones or the bark of living trees. As most places have been found by an experienced druid at some point in history, you can expect places of intrigue to have such messages near their entrance. <br>
*As GM, please ask "are there any Druidic Markings here?" from time to time. I might forget, but they're supposed to be a hint system.*

### Level 5: Nature's/Natural/Primal Ward?
When a Beast ot Plant creature would make an Attack against you, it must make a Charisma Save against your Passive Magic Skill. <br>
On a failed Save, the attacking creature must choose a different target, or the attack automatically misses. <br>
On a successful Save, the creature is immune to this effect for the day. <br>
The creature is aware of this effect as soon as it is aware of you.

### Level 7: Gift of the Wild?
You may at any point call to mind one Spell from the *Land's Champion* table and cast it as if you knew it as a Circle Spell, expending a Spell Slot as normal. <br>
You can use this feature once per Adventure. (PHRASING?)
