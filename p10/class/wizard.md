## Wizard Subclass: Chaos Theory
THEME: Magic is chaotic by default, and spells are just ways to tame its power. It must SURELY be more effective to just let it all loose, right?

### Level 1: Imbue with Potential
Take and hour and spend MP up to your Mana Spend Limit to store a spark in an object for up to a day.<br>
A creature can use the object to release the spark, causing a Wild Magic Surge with a Surge Value equal to 2 times the MP spent when it was stored.<br>
A single object can't hold more than 1 spark at a time.

### Level 1: Arcane Flux
You can use 1 Action on your turn and expend a Spell Slot to cause Wild Magic Surge with a Surge Value equal to the Rank of the expended Spell Slot. <br>
After doing so, you may regain one Spell Slot of a lower rank than the one you expended.

### Level 2: Reflexive Flux
**Prerequisite:** *Arcane Flux*<br>
After you take damage, you can use your *Arcane Flux* regardless of whose turn it is, even if the damage is lethal and Death's Door would otherwise deny you the Action.

## Wizard Subclass: School of Abjuration
THEME: Some fight fire with fire. You fire with an extinguisher. Your approach is highly efficient.

### Level 1: Abjuration Savant
You learn 2 Arcane Abjuration Spells.<br>
The time and cost required to make a Spell Scroll is halved, provided the Spell you bind to it is an Abjuration Spell.

### Level 1: Counterspell
If you can see a Spell be successfully cast by a source no more than 1 pace outside your Throwing Range, you can use 1 Action and spend MP equal to the amount spent on that casting to negate its effects. You gain Excess MP equal to the non-excess MP you spend this way.
